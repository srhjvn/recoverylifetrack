## Purpose
Create a single, organized medical documentation packet that supports a disability benefits application and helps reduce missing-information delays.

---

## Definition of Done
- formal mental health evaluation is scheduled or completed
- provider list is created
- all known treatment sources are identified
- requested records are logged
- received records are stored in one place
- symptom/function summary is drafted
- medication list is current

---

## Core Principle
The goal is not just to collect records. The goal is to collect the **right records**, know what is still missing, and keep the material organized enough to use under stress.

---

## Document Categories

### 1. Identity and Provider Information
- full legal name
- date of birth
- phone number
- mailing address
- emergency contact if needed
- provider names
- provider specialties
- clinic or hospital names
- provider phone and fax numbers
- dates of treatment

### 2. Diagnostic Documentation
- formal diagnostic evaluation
- psychiatric evaluation
- psychological assessment
- discharge summary if applicable
- diagnosis update notes
- written statement of current conditions

### 3. Treatment Records
- therapy notes or treatment summaries
- psychiatry follow-up notes
- primary care notes related to mental health symptoms
- crisis visits
- emergency room visits
- inpatient or outpatient program notes
- referrals to specialists

### 4. Medication Documentation
- current medication list
- prior medication list if relevant
- dosage history
- prescribing provider
- side effects that affect functioning
- medication changes over time

### 5. Functional Impact Documentation
- how symptoms affect concentration
- how symptoms affect memory
- how symptoms affect pace and consistency
- how symptoms affect reliability and attendance
- how symptoms affect ability to leave home
- how symptoms affect communication or social interaction
- how symptoms affect stress tolerance
- how symptoms affect household functioning and routine

## Symptom / Function Summary Drafting Guide

Use this section to create a plain-language summary you can later adapt for forms, appointments, or representation support.

### A. Condition Overview
- when symptoms became severe enough to disrupt normal functioning
- whether symptoms are continuous, recurring, or episodic
- whether symptoms have worsened over time

### B. Daily Functioning
- getting out of bed
- maintaining hygiene
- keeping appointments
- cooking or preparing food
- managing paperwork
- managing phone calls or messages
- shopping or transportation
- maintaining a routine

### C. Work-Related Impact
- concentration difficulties
- task completion problems
- inability to sustain pace
- attendance inconsistency
- overwhelm during normal workplace demands
- difficulty handling change, pressure, or deadlines
- reduced ability to interact with others reliably

### D. Treatment Efforts
- providers seen
- therapies tried
- medications tried
- hospital or crisis interventions
- what improved, if anything
- what remains limiting

## Risks
- provider wait times
- incomplete treatment history
- forgotten providers or treatment dates
- records offices delaying release
- difficulty summarizing symptoms clearly under stress

---

## Mitigation
- request earliest available appointments
- write down every provider remembered, even if dates are rough
- use the tracker for all requests and follow-ups
- save everything immediately when received
- update the summary in small pieces rather than all at once
