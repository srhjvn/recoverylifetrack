## Purpose
Create a plain-language summary of symptoms, limitations, and day-to-day functional impact that can support appointments, forms, legal intake, and disability application preparation.

---

## Definition of Done
- major symptoms are described in plain language
- daily functioning impact is documented
- work-related limitations are documented
- treatment efforts are summarized
- the summary is specific enough to reuse in multiple settings

---

## Working Principle
This file is not about sounding dramatic. It is about being concrete, consistent, and honest about what functioning looks like on real days.

---

## Writing Guidance
- describe what happens, not just what the diagnosis is
- use examples from daily life where possible
- note frequency, intensity, and recovery time when relevant
- distinguish between a typical day and a bad day
- avoid minimizing limits just because they are familiar

## Important Reminders While Drafting
- symptoms do not have to be constant to be disabling
- the issue is whether functioning is reliable and sustainable
- describe what happens repeatedly, not just the best moments
- short bursts of functioning do not erase the impact of crashes, episodes, or inconsistency
